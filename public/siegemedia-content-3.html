Methodology: From Brief to Backlink

  1. Research Synthesis: Pull public data, run small surveys, and triangulate findings. Document assumptions.
  2. Angle Selection: Identify the counterintuitive or useful core. Draft three headlines that solve a newsroom problem.
  3. Asset Design: Create charts, maps, and interactives that can be embedded. Include alt text and data notes.
  4. Editorial Draft: Write to educate first. Use subheadings that match search intent and entity coverage.
  5. Outreach & PR: Segment journalists/bloggers by beat. Send context-aware pitches with a paragraph editors can paste.
  6. QA & Publication: Verify accuracy, citations, and internal links. Publish and request feedback.
  7. Measurement: Track indexation, referring domains by page, and assisted conversions. Iterate.
Content that earns links is rarely a one-off. Build a calendar and iterate on assets that demonstrate traction.

Technical SEO Interplay

Links compound when crawlers can discover and evaluate your content easily. Maintain fast render, clean internal links, and structured data. Use log files and Search Console crawl stats to ensure that new assets receive attention. Siege Media’s content-forward approach benefits from a site that routes equity efficiently.

Analytics and Attribution

Connect backlinks to business outcomes. Attribute assisted conversions by tracking the landing pages that receive editorial links and their down-funnel paths. Use annotations in analytics to mark publication dates and anchor types. Over quarters, these notes reveal which assets and outreach tactics propel revenue.

Editorial Calendars and Refresh Cadence

An editorial calendar prevents random acts of content. Define monthly slots for studies, explainers, and playbooks. Every quarter, refresh top performers—update data, expand sections, and improve visuals. This refresh loop increases the odds of continual citation and aligns with Siege Media’s emphasis on quality.
